## Key Business Insights from the Data

### Seasonality Patterns
- **Q4 is your strongest quarter** — average weekly sales of $16,642
  compared to $7,000 in Q1. November and December are peak months.
- **September spike** — average weekly sales jump to $18,045 in September,
  likely driven by back-to-school and corporate procurement cycles.
- **February is the weakest month** — average weekly sales of $4,317.
  This is a natural trough and a good period for inventory planning.

### Most Predictable Sales Drivers
The model identified these as the strongest signals for forecasting:

1. **Month of year** — seasonality is the dominant factor
2. **Week of year** — weekly patterns within each season
3. **Last week's sales** — recent momentum carries forward
4. **Same week last year** — year-over-year patterns repeat reliably

## How to Use This Forecast for Business Planning

### Inventory Management
- Stock conservatively in January–February based on the forecast trough
- Begin building inventory in August to prepare for the September surge
- Heaviest stock-up should happen before November

### Cash Flow Planning
- Use the 12-week projected revenue to plan operating expenses
- Low Q1 forecasts signal a period to conserve cash, not expand

### Staffing
- Reduce temporary staffing in Q1 (post-holiday wind-down)
- Begin hiring or training seasonal staff in October ahead of Q4 peak

### Promotions
- February and January are ideal months for clearance promotions
- Avoid heavy discounting in September, November, December
  when demand is naturally high
